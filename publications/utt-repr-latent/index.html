Learning utterance-level representations through token-level acoustic latents prediction for Expressive Speech Synthesis

Karolos Nikitaras, Konstantinos Klapsas, Nikolaos Ellinas, Georgia Maniati, June Sig Sung, Inchul Hwang, Aimilios Chalamandaris, Pirros Tsiakoulis and Spyros Raptis

Abstract: This paper proposes a multi-scale latent variable, text-to-speech (TTS) model for Expressive Speech Synthesis.Our goal is to capture and control utterance-level attributes within a system that performs token-level prosody modeling. Current works aim to factorize different resolution speech attributes into representations extracted by modules that operate in the corresponding scale. We show that fine-grained latent space itself is able to capture coarse-grained information, such as character acting voice and style. Therefore, it arises a trade-off between the diversity of the different resolution representations and their disentanglement We alleviate such issue by first capturing multi-resolution speech attributes into a token-level latent space and then, separately train a prior network that, given a text, learns utterance-level representations in order to predict the phoneme-level, posterior latents extracted during the previous step. Both qualitative and quantitative evaluations are used to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Model Architecture

Audio samples are named in the form: <model_name>_<u><number>_<f><number>   where

  • model_name is proposed (prop), hierarchical baseline (hbase), global baseline (gbase) or a simple Non-Attentive (nat)
  • u_(utterance-level dims) is the number of latent dimensions used by Reference Encoder
  • f_(fine-grained dims) is the number of latent dimensions used by FVAE
