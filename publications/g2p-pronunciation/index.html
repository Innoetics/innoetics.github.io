Sequence-to-Sequence G2P-driven Compilation of Pronunciation Lexicon for Out-of-Vocabulary Words

Georgia Maniati, Aimilios Chalamandaris, Georgios Vamvoukakis, Nikolaos Ellinas, Panos Kakoulidis, Georgios Dimitriou, Konstantinos Markopoulos, Pirros Tsiakoulis, Spyros Raptis, Gunu Jho, June Sig Sung, Jihyeok Yoon, Hyoungmin Park

Abstract: In this paper, we propose a method for compiling large and accurate pronunciation lexica for out-of-vocabulary words for text-to-speech synthesis. The method combines a neural sequence-to-sequence grapheme-to-phoneme (G2P) model, a state-of-the-art text-to-speech system and crowdsourced evaluation. For each out-of-vocabulary word, we generate multiple alternative transcriptions using the G2P model and then convert them into speech. The synthesized transcriptions are then validated using a Preference Test by multiple non-linguistic-expert users via Amazon Mechanical Turk. The alternatives that exceed a threshold majority of preference among the listeners are considered correct and added to the pronunciation lexicon. The entire process is iterated by first retraining the neural G2P model to boost its performance, and then evaluating additional out-of-vocabulary words. Through this process we can quickly create large crowdsourced lexica that improve our resources' quality and the neural G2P robustness.
