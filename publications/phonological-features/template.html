Phonological Features for Cross-lingual Text-to-Speech and Speaker Adaptation with Limited Data

Georgia Maniati*Equal Contribution, Nikolaos Ellinas*Equal Contribution, Konstantinos Markopoulos, Georgios Vamvoukakis, June Sig Sung, Hyoungmin Park, Aimilios Chalamandaris and Pirros Tsiakoulis

Abstract: The idea of using phonological features instead of phonemes as input to sequence-to-sequence TTS has been recently proposed for zero-shot multilingual speech synthesis. This approach is especially useful for code-switched speech as it facilitates the seamless uttering of foreign text embedded in a stream of na- tive text. In our work, we train a multispeaker Tacotron-based model conditioned on a set of phonologically derived features which is common across different languages without the addi- tion of language embeddings, aiming at language-agnostic TTS. This method enables us to generate intelligible synthetic speech with the voice of every training speaker in any seen or unseen language. We present experiments on the effect of phonetic coverage on different train-test language combinations. Fine- tuning with very limited data of a new speaker’s voice in ei- ther a seen or an unseen language, enables the model to gen- erate synthetic speech of equal quality, while preserving the target speaker’s identity. We experiment on the amount of tar- get speaker data and achieve acceptable naturalness and speaker similarity scores with 32 and 8 utterances. We also show that in the extreme case of only 2 available utterances, both in the case of seen and unseen languages the performance is similar.
